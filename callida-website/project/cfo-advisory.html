How we think about it

Most finance challenges aren't really about the numbers — they're about how the numbers are made, shared and used. We work across all three.

Understand 01

Diagnose the finance function as it really operates.

Before we design anything, we map how decisions actually get made — where the financial signal is strong, where it's lost, and what the executive really needs to see.

  • Financial strategy and operating model support.

    Aligning the finance function to the agency's portfolio, accountabilities and Estimates rhythm.

  • Cost transparency and decision-support models.

    Activity-based cost models, scenario tools and unit-economics views that survive contact with executive questions.

Shape 02

Build the analysis and options the executive can actually use.

Practical models, evidence and forward views — designed for the cycle of Budget, MYEFO, Additional Estimates and audit.

  • Budgeting, forecasting and performance reporting.

    End-to-end budgeting frameworks, rolling forecasts and performance reporting fit for portfolio scale.

  • Executive reporting and stakeholder engagement.

    Clear, trusted reporting that bridges finance, delivery and policy — and works in the brief, the board and the committee.

Deliver 03

Embed governance and improvement that holds after we leave.

We hand over working tools, documented frameworks and a stronger team — not a set of slides nobody can run.

  • Assurance, governance and financial improvement initiatives.

    Independent review, control improvement and the design of governance that the second line and Audit Committee can trust.

  • Capability transfer to client teams.

    Every engagement is structured to leave the client's people more capable than we found them.

03 — How we work

A measured rhythm. No theatre.

Five movements that every CFO Advisory engagement follows. We adapt the depth, not the discipline.

  1. 01

    Understand the operating context.

    We immerse in the agency, the portfolio, the stakeholders and the calendar before we recommend anything.

  2. 02

    Diagnose issues and opportunities.

    A clear, evidence-based view of what's working, what isn't, and where the executive should focus first.

  3. 03

    Build practical options and evidence.

    Models, scenarios and recommendations designed for executive decision-making — not internal whiteboards.

  4. 04

    Support implementation and adoption.

    We stay through the awkward middle — when frameworks meet reality and the work needs senior hands.

  5. 05

    Transfer knowledge to client teams.

    Documented, runnable, and owned by your team. We're not the contingent staff plan.
